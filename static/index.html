Why adversarial reasoning works

The legal system discovered this centuries ago: truth is more reliable when every claim must survive a skilled challenge. Science calls it peer review. Philosophy calls it dialectics. We've never applied it to AI — until now.

The problem with regular AI answers

Every AI response is a closing argument with no opposing counsel. The model optimizes for sounding correct, not being correct. Nothing forces it to confront what it might be wrong about.

Use the lenses

The same question looks different through an economist's eyes vs. a philosopher's. Switch lenses to see which arguments survive across worldviews — those are the ones worth taking seriously.
